Beginner Tips

Feeling overwhelmed or confused?
Read on!

How do I build a custom model?

Simply click on "Workshop", and fill out each tab (General, Batting, Baserunning, Pitching, and Fielding). Then go to the "Submit" tab, pick your variance, and click RUN THE MODEL!

What do these stats mean?

In Workshop, we give a concise description of each stat, but a complete description can typically be found with a quick google search. We offer an extremely wide range of available statistics, many of which only the most dedicated fans have heard about. To get started, there is no harm in sticking with the stats you know.

What does “3-year weighted average” mean?

Computer models typically use three-year weighted averages in order to more accurately predict future results. This means considering the past three seasons of data, while more heavily weighing more recent seasons. A model relying solely on current-season data may be more subject to small sample sizes and random fluctuations.

What are plus stats?

A "plus" stat is a stat that is adjusted for ballparks. For example, a Colorado batter may appear to be better solely because they play half their games in a hitter-friendly environment. Only some stats, such as OBP and SLG, have plus alternatives.

What is variance?

Variance is a measure of randomness. With a variance value of 0, a model would give the favorite a 100% chance of winning every single time. With an extremely high variance value, a model would give the favorite a ~50% chance of winning every single time. It may take experimentation to find the right variance value for your custom model.

How does it all work?

You can use the model without understanding how the model operates. That being said, for a complete explanation of how everything works, see the HOW IT WORKS page.
